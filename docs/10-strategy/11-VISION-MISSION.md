## McKinsey Layered Communication Model

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ TAGLINE (3-7 words)                                                 │
│ Purpose: Memorability                                               │
│ Audience: Everyone                                                  │
│ "Your biology. Our wisdom. Community."                              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ MISSION (15-25 words)                                               │
│ Purpose: Direction + Differentiation                                │
│ Audience: Employees, investors                                      │
│ "We unite your biology with every medical tradition..."             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ VALUE PROPOSITION (2-3 sentences)                                   │
│ Purpose: Full mechanism                                             │
│ Audience: Customers making decisions                                │
│ "If conventional medicine has left you without answers..."          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Vision Statement

### Final Statement ✅

> **Building a world where everyone can take charge of their physical and mental health.**

### Analysis

| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Purpose** | Aspirational future state |
| **Durability** | 10+ years relevant |
| **Inspirational** | Yes — empowerment, universal |
| **Scope** | Physical AND mental health |
| **Action Implied** | "Take charge" — agency, control |

### Why This Works

- **"Building a world"** — active, ongoing construction (not "create" which implies completion)
- **"Everyone"** — universal aspiration, not limited to sick people
- **"Take charge"** — empowerment language, user agency
- **"Physical and mental"** — explicitly includes mental health (founder's motivation includes depression, anxiety, cognitive issues)

## Mission Statement

### Final Statement ✅

> **We unite your biology with every medical tradition, so you can heal, thrive, and find the help and community you have been looking for.**

(24 words)

### McKinsey Evaluation Framework

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Customer-first | 30% | Leads with customer outcome, not internal activity |
| Differentiation | 25% | Captures what makes you unique (the moat) |
| Durability | 20% | Will still be relevant in 10 years |
| Memorability | 15% | Can employees and customers recall it? |
| Decision-guiding | 10% | Helps say "no" to things that don't fit |

### Final Scoring

| Criterion | Weight | Score | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------|
| Customer-first | 30% | 10 | "Your biology" + "you have been looking for" = deeply empathetic |
| Differentiation | 25% | 8 | "Unite every medical tradition" + help + community |
| Durability | 20% | 9 | Timeless, no tech jargon |
| Memorability | 15% | 7.5 | 24 words (longer), but emotionally sticky ending |
| Decision-guiding | 10% | 8.5 | Does it unite traditions? Provide help? Build community? |
| **Weighted Total** | | **8.7** | |

### Why This Works

| Element | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **"Unite"** | Stronger than "connect" or "unify", implies bringing disparate things together |
| **"Your biology"** | Immediately centers the customer, personal |
| **"Every medical tradition"** | States the moat clearly (pharma + TCM + Ayurveda + Kampo + etc.) |
| **"Heal, thrive"** | Outcome-focused, covers both fixing problems AND flourishing |
| **"You have been looking for"** | Validates the user's journey, acknowledges their search |

### Structural Clarity

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Outcomes | Heal, thrive |
| Mechanism | Find the help and community you have been looking for |

No conflation — outcomes are separate from how you achieve them.

## Mission = Company Action (Not World State)

### Distinction from Vision

| Layer | Structure | Focus |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Vision** | "Building a world where..." | Aspirational world state |
| **Mission** | "We connect/build/make..." | Company action |

Vision describes WHERE we're going. Mission describes WHAT we do.

---

## Platform Dual-Mode Model

The mission must reflect that the platform is MORE than a marketplace:

| Mode | Description | Pricing | User Need |
|------|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **Self-Service Research** | Users explore unified knowledge library with their own health data. DIY health research. | Free or very affordable | "I want to understand my own biology" |
| **Marketplace** | Connect with practitioners, buy report templates, join peer communities | Transactional (% fees) | "I want expert help or community support" |

### Why This Matters

- Serves the founder's mission: help the "unhelped" who can't afford practitioners
- Lowers barrier to entry (freemium model)
- Knowledge library IS the core value; marketplace layers on top
- Self-service users may convert to marketplace users over time

---

## The 9s Paradox

**Why perfect 10s across all criteria are rare:**

| Criterion | Pulls Toward |
|-----------|--------------|
| Memorability | Brevity |
| Differentiation | Specificity |
| Decision-guiding | Operational detail |

These are in tension. A perfectly memorable statement may lack specificity. A highly differentiated statement may be too long.

**Solution:** Accept 8-8.5 on mission, push for 9s on tagline.
