## Summary

EMA herbal monographs and assessment reports are official regulatory documents in the public domain. They can be freely referenced and used for both academic and commercial purposes. Citation of the source is recommended for professional use.

---

## Permissions

What you CAN do with this data:

- Reference for regulatory submissions
- Use in commercial product documentation
- Cite in academic publications
- Download and redistribute monographs
- Use for safety and efficacy assessments

---

## Restrictions

What you CANNOT do without additional permission:

- Claim EMA as endorsing specific products
- Misrepresent regulatory status
- Use EMA branding without permission

---

## Special Terms

### Regulatory Authority

EMA monographs represent official EU regulatory assessments. They define conditions for well-established use and traditional use registrations.

### Well-Established vs Traditional Use

- **Well-Established Use**: Supported by clinical evidence
- **Traditional Use**: Based on >=30 years of use (>=15 in EU)

### European Pharmacopoeia

Quality standards reference the European Pharmacopoeia, which has separate access terms.
