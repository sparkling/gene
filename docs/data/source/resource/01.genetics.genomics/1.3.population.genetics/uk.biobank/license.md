## Summary

UK Biobank data access requires a formal application and approval process. All users must sign the Material Transfer Agreement (MTA) and adhere to strict data handling requirements. Commercial use is permitted but requires explicit approval. Data cannot be transferred to third parties or used beyond the approved research scope.

---

## Permissions

What you CAN do with this data:

- Conduct approved research projects
- Publish research findings with proper citation
- Access genetic and phenotypic data within approved scope
- Use Research Analysis Platform for analysis

---

## Restrictions

What you CANNOT do without additional permission:

- Access data without approved application
- Transfer data to unauthorized parties
- Use data beyond approved research purposes
- Retain data after project completion
- Commercial use without specific approval
- Attempt to re-identify participants

---

## Special Terms

### Application Requirements

UK Biobank access requires:
1. Research application with scientific rationale
2. Institutional affiliation
3. Ethics approval (if applicable)
4. Signed Material Transfer Agreement
5. Access fees (for some data types)
6. Annual renewal for continued access

### Data Return Policy

Research results and derived data may need to be returned to UK Biobank before publication.

### Access Tiers

| Data Type | Additional Requirements |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Standard phenotypes | Basic approval |
| Genetic data | Standard + genetic terms |
| Imaging | Additional imaging approval |
| Primary care | Additional GP data approval |
| Return of Results | Additional ethics |
